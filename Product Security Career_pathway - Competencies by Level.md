# Product Security Career Pathway: Competencies by Level

## Career Level Comparison Table

| **Dimension** | **Associate/Junior** | **Mid-Level** | **Senior** |
|---------------|---------------------|---------------|------------|
| **Problem Solving & Initiative** | Tackles well-defined, simple tasks within confined scope. Troubleshoots simpler issues and debugs small code sections. Provides incomplete or surface-level remediation guidance. Technically limited on operational tasks. | Proactively identifies security issues and proposes comprehensive solutions. Handles moderate complexity problems independently across broader scope. | Thinks ahead and prevents security issues before they happen. Handles complex, multifaceted problems independently. Devises innovative solutions to reduce attack surface while ensuring no customer impact. |
| **Strategic Thinking** | Very micro task-oriented, cannot see the macro of why work is done. Curious but unable to understand the bigger picture. Executes tasks right away before fully understanding context or purpose. | Beginning to see connections between tasks and broader goals. Considers project-level impact. Takes time to understand "why" before execution. | Thinks "why" first - understands the bigger picture (Macro) before diving into details (Micro). Connects security initiatives to business objectives. Analyzes purpose before taking action. |
| **Autonomy & Guidance** | Cannot handle large scope projects. Needs step-by-step guidance and detailed plans with every part clearly written. Requires consistent routine touchpoints. Follows established procedures and seeks approvals for major decisions. | Works independently on projects with periodic check-ins. Creates project plans with some guidance. Escalates appropriately when blocked. | Handles projects start-to-finish with minimal assistance. Creates robust project plans with clear communication. Only escalates when blocked by issues beyond their scope. |
| **Technical Depth** | Technically limited on operational tasks. Learning security fundamentals and tool usage. Applies known techniques to standard scenarios. Recipient of guidance - attends workshops to enhance skills. | Solid understanding of security principles. Can apply techniques across various scenarios with adaptation. Begins to develop specialized expertise in specific areas. | Deep technical expertise across multiple domains. Actively researches security trends and learns new techniques. Seeks advanced knowledge and passive learning opportunities. Leads workshops and knowledge-sharing initiatives. |
| **Communication & Influence** | Communicates within immediate team, sharing updates on smaller tasks (e.g., discussing a false positive ticket). Focuses on doing work rather than building internal and external connections and relationships. Still developing ability to explain security concepts simply. | Explains security issues clearly to technical teams. Actively builds cross-functional relationships. Beginning to influence team-level decisions and communicate with non-technical stakeholders. | Simplifies and explains complex technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders, including executives and clients. Influences decision-makers and shapes security culture across the organization. |
| **Risk Management** | Understands compliance requirements but unable to contextualize and extract what needs to be done for team to meet them. Identifies risks with significant support. Follows established risk frameworks without adaptation. | Assesses risk with growing independence. Contextualizes compliance requirements and translates them into actionable steps. Recommends mitigation strategies tailored to business context. | Balances security and business needs expertly. Takes calculated risks and makes informed trade-off decisions independently. Translates complex compliance requirements into clear, actionable team objectives. |
| **Ownership & Accountability** | Owns individual assigned tasks. Completes work with oversight. Worried about providing value immediately rather than taking time to do it right. Needs supervisor consent to introduce changes. | Owns small-to-medium projects. Takes responsibility for deliverables within their scope. Balances speed with quality. Makes project decisions with appropriate stakeholder involvement. | Owns major security initiatives end-to-end. Ensures follow-through and holds teams accountable for security measures. Drives outcomes. Prioritizes doing work correctly over rushing to show immediate value. |
| **Mentorship & Leadership** | Recipient of mentorship. Seeks learning opportunities and attends workshops. Collaborates with team members on assigned tasks. | Begins mentoring junior team members. Provides guidance on familiar topics. Leads small technical initiatives or working groups. | Actively mentors and develops junior and mid-level engineers. Leads coding workshops and knowledge-sharing initiatives. Sets technical direction and standards for the team. |
| **Scope of Impact** | Individual contributor on specific tasks. Impact within immediate work area. | Project-level impact. Influences team practices and tooling decisions. | Organization-wide impact. Shapes security strategy, standards, and culture across multiple teams. |
| **Decision Making** | Follows established guidelines and seeks approvals for major decisions. Needs supervisor consent to introduce new features or changes. Seeks validation frequently before taking action. | Makes confident decisions within project scope. Involves stakeholders appropriately. Requires less frequent approval for routine decisions. | Makes high-stakes security and architectural decisions independently based on wealth of experience. Makes critical architecture choices without needing approval. Trusted advisor to leadership. |
| **Incident Response** | Supports incident response under guidance. Documents findings and follows runbooks. | Leads response for moderate incidents. Conducts analysis and creates remediation plans. | Leads critical incident response. Orchestrates cross-team coordination. Conducts post-mortems and drives systemic improvements. |
| **Process & Tooling** | Uses existing security tools and processes. Suggests improvements based on experience. | Improves and optimizes existing processes. Evaluates and recommends new tools. | Designs security processes and frameworks. Architects tooling strategies. Champions security automation and scalability. |
| **Collaboration** | Works well within their immediate team. Building cross-functional relationships. | Collaborates effectively across teams. Partners with engineering, product, and infrastructure teams. | Builds strategic partnerships across the organization. Influences product roadmaps and engineering practices. Represents security in executive discussions. |
| **Meeting Participation & Representation** | Does not attend critical meetings or is a "fly on the wall" observer. Limited participation in strategic discussions. | Attends important meetings and contributes meaningfully. Beginning to represent the team in cross-functional forums. | Key point of contact that mimics program owner's goals and outlook. Serves as stand-in for manager/director. Covers critical meetings and makes decisions on behalf of leadership. |

## Additional Senior Qualities

Beyond the core competencies listed above, Senior AppSec/Product Security professionals typically demonstrate:

- **Thought Leadership**: Represents the company at conferences, writes blog posts, contributes to open-source security projects
- **Business Acumen**: Understands revenue impact, prioritizes based on business value, speaks the language of executives
- **Crisis Management**: Remains calm under pressure, makes sound decisions during security incidents
- **Continuous Improvement**: Drives security maturity initiatives, implements metrics and KPIs to measure program effectiveness
- **Vendor Management**: Evaluates and manages relationships with security vendors and external partners
- **Compliance Expertise**: Navigates regulatory requirements (SOC2, ISO, GDPR, etc.) and translates them into actionable requirements
- **Adaptability**: Pivots strategies based on changing threat landscape, business priorities, or organizational shifts
